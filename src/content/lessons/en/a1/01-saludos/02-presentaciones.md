To introduce yourself in Basque, a very simple formula is enough: **Ni** (I) + your name + **naiz** (am).

## The pattern

| Basque | English |
|---|---|
| *Ni Egoitz naiz.* | I am Egoitz. |
| *Ni Maialen naiz.* | I am Maialen. |
| *Ni Hodei naiz.* | I am Hodei. |

The verb **izan** ("to be") changes according to the person. For now, just memorize three forms:

- *Ni* + **naiz** → I am
- *Zu* + **zara** → you are
- *Hura* + **da** → he/she is

> In Basque there is no grammatical gender in the verb: *Hura da* works the same for "he is" or "she is". The context or the name makes it clear.

## "How are you?"

Once you have introduced yourself, the natural thing is to ask how the other person is doing:

- *Zer moduz?* → How are you?
- *Ondo, eskerrik asko.* → Fine, thank you.
- *Ni ere ondo.* → I'm fine too.

With those three phrases you can already keep up a complete, polite greeting.
