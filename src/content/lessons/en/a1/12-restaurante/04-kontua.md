The end of the meal has its own little script: ask for the bill, pay and say goodbye.

## Asking for the bill

Three ways, all correct:

| Euskera | English | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| **Kontua, mesedez.** | The bill, please. | neutral, clear |
| **Atera kontua, mesedez.** | Bring me the bill, please. | neutral |
| **Kobratuko al didazu?** | Will you charge me? | polite (future) |

`Kobratuko al didazu?` is heard all the time in informal bars and restaurants. *Al* is a question particle and *didazu* is "(you to) me" (NOR-NORI-NORK).

## The total price

- **Zenbat da dena?** — How much is everything?
- **Hamabost euro eta hogei zentimo.** — Fifteen euros and twenty cents.

## Paying

| Euskera | English |
|---|---|
| *Txartelaz ordain dezaket?* | Can I pay by card? |
| *Eskudirutan ordainduko dut.* | I'll pay in cash. |
| *Tori!* | Here you go! (when handing over the money) |
| *Hemen daukazu kanbioa.* | Here is your change. |
| *Ez daukat kanbiorik.* | I have no change. |

> *Eskudiru* = "money at hand" → cash. *Txartel* = card. *Kanbio* is a loanword and is used quite normally.

## Tips

In Euskal Herria **tipping is not mandatory**. What is usual:

- In bars and informal meals: leaving the loose coins from your change (50 cents to 1 euro).
- In restaurants with a higher bill: 1-2 euros per person if you enjoyed it.
- The typical 10% in the style of other countries is never required.

## Saying goodbye

- **Eskerrik asko, oso ondo zegoen!** — Thank you very much, it was delicious!
- **Zuri! Agur!** — Thank you! Goodbye!
- **Hurrengora arte!** — Until next time!

## Full mini-sequence

> — *Aizu, kobratuko al didazu, mesedez?*
>
> — *Bai, banoa. Hamazortzi euro eta berrogeita hamar zentimo.*
>
> — *Txartelaz ordain dezaket?*
>
> — *Bai, noski.*
>
> — *Eskerrik asko! Oso ondo zegoen.*
>
> — *Zuri! Agur!*

> **Cultural note:** leaving the coin from your coffee as a small tip for the waiter is a fairly common gesture in Basque bars, especially if you stay for a while to chat. It's not expected, but it's appreciated.
