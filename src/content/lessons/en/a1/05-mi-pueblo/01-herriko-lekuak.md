Before you can give directions, you need the basic vocabulary to talk about the neighbourhood (**auzoa**) and the town (**herria**). In this lesson we gather the most typical places and services.

## Shops and commerce

| Basque | English |
|---|---|
| *taberna* | bar |
| *kafetegia* | café |
| *jatetxea* | restaurant |
| *okindegia* | bakery |
| *harategia* | butcher's |
| *arrandegia* | fishmonger's |
| *fruta-denda* | greengrocer's |
| *gozoki-denda* | sweet shop |
| *merkatua* / *supermerkatua* | market / supermarket |
| *jantzi-denda* | clothes shop |
| *liburu-denda* | bookshop |
| *loradenda* | florist's |

> Many words follow the pattern **product + denda** ("shop of…"): *fruta-denda*, *liburu-denda*, *jantzi-denda*. If you know the product, you know the place.

## The suffix -tegi and friends

Notice these productive suffixes: they come up again and again.

- **-tegi** → place where something is: *euskal·tegi* (place of Basque), *ile-apain·degi* (place where hair is done), *liburu·tegi* (place of books).
- **-degi / -tegi** → variant with the same idea: *gasolin·degi*, *okin·degi*.
- **-etxe** ("house") → *udal·etxe* (house of the council), *osasun·etxe* (house of health), *jatetxe* (place of eating).

> **Cultural note:** in many Basque neighbourhoods and towns the **taberna** and the **plaza** are the gathering spots par excellence, and the **frontoia** (fronton) is usually next to the church. A *euskaltegi* is precisely the school where you are learning this language.
